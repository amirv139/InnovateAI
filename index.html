For Discrete Manufacturing

For Human Workers

A camera watches a workstation as a worker begins a task. Our AI analyzes the visual data and provides real-time, interactive instructions on a connected screen. It shows the worker exactly where to place a part and instantly validates that the step was done correctly. This turns a complex assembly routing into a simple, error-proof process, which drastically reduces training time for new employees.

For Robots

For robots, our software is the intelligent brain that allows them to move beyond a pre-programmed path. The AI analyzes the position of a new part and uses pose estimation to determine its exact location and orientation in 3D space. It then communicates with the robot's controller, providing a dynamic and precise path for its arm to assemble the part. This removes the need for rigid jigs and fixtures, making the robot highly adaptable to product changes.

For Process Manufacturing

For Human Workers

In a food factory, our AI watches a conveyor belt of incoming ingredients. It provides real-time guidance to a worker on what to look for, such as a defect on a piece of meat or an improperly shaped vegetable. A connected screen highlights the item and instructs the worker on the precise action to take, allowing them to make perfect cuts to maximize yield and reduce waste.

For Robots

Our AI gives a robot the ability to adapt to natural variations. For a packaging robot, the AI uses computer vision to inspect every item coming down the line. It analyzes a bag of snacks, for example, to ensure it is sealed correctly and has a perfect label. If a defect is found, the AI instantly instructs the robot to remove the flawed product from the line, ensuring that only high-quality items make it to the customer.
